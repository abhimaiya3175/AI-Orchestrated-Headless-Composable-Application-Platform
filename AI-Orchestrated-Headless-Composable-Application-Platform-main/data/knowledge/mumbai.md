# Mumbai Travel Guide

## Overview
Mumbai, India's financial capital and film industry hub (Bollywood). A city of contrasts — luxury high-rises next to century-old colonial architecture. Incredible street food, vibrant nightlife, and iconic landmarks. Can be visited year-round but best October–February.

## Best Time to Visit
- **October–February**: Dry, 20–32°C, no humidity, perfect
- **March–May**: Hot (32-38°C), manageable
- **June–September**: Monsoon — heavy rains, flooding possible but scenic

## Iconic Attractions
- **Gateway of India**: Built in 1924, arch monument on the waterfront, base for Elephanta Caves ferries
- **Marine Drive (Queen's Necklace)**: 3.5km promenade along the sea, magical at night
- **Elephanta Caves**: UNESCO caves on an island (1hr ferry from Gateway), ancient rock-cut temples
- **Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT)**: UNESCO railway station, Gothic Victorian architecture
- **Haji Ali Dargah**: Mosque on a tiny islet, only accessible at low tide — stunning
- **Siddhivinayak Temple**: One of the most visited Hindu shrines in India

## Neighborhoods & Markets
- **Colaba**: Touristy hub, Colaba Causeway market for souvenirs, cafés
- **Bandra (W)**: Hipster neighborhood, best restaurants and bars, luxury shopping
- **Dharavi**: Asia's largest slum, fascinating walking tours show Mumbai's resilience
- **Crawford Market**: Fresh produce, spices, pets — experience local life
- **Linking Road & Hill Road**: Best budget shopping in the city

## Food
- **Vada Pav**: Mumbai's burger — spiced potato patty in a bun, ₹15–25
- **Pav Bhaji**: Buttery vegetable curry with bread, best at Juhu Beach
- **Bhel Puri & Sev Puri**: Mumbai's signature street chaat
- **Bombay Duck (Bombay Fish)**: Deep-fried lizardfish — a local delicacy
- **Irani Chai**: Century-old Parsi-style tea cafés like Kyani & Co.
- **Dabba System**: Mumbai's iconic tiffin delivery — witness it at CST station around noon

## Budget Guide
- **Budget**: ₹3,000–5,000/day (hostel + local trains + street food)
- **Mid-range**: ₹5,000–10,000/day (3-star hotel + restaurants + Ola)
- **Luxury**: ₹10,000–30,000/day (South Mumbai 5-star + fine dining)
- **Hotels are expensive**: Book in advance, especially in Colaba or Bandra

## Transport
- **Local trains**: Best and cheapest (₹5–15), get a day pass
- **Metro**: Newer, air-conditioned, limited lines but expanding
- **Auto/Taxi**: Use Ola or Uber for fair pricing
- **Ferry**: Gateway → Elephanta, Alibaug, Mandwa

## Tips & Advice
- Mumbai runs 24/7 — it truly never sleeps
- Street food in Juhu and Chowpatty Beach areas is safest and most authentic
- Avoid peak hours (8–10am / 5–8pm) on local trains — impossible crowds
- Negotiate rates at Colaba Causeway stalls, standard discount is 40-50%
- Monsoon is atmospheric but bring waterproof gear — streets flood fast
