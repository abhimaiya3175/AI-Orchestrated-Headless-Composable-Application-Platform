# Goa Travel Guide

## Overview
Goa is India's smallest state located on the western coast. Famous for its beaches, Portuguese heritage, vibrant nightlife, and seafood cuisine. Best visited between October and March when the weather is dry and pleasant.

## Best Time to Visit
- **October–March**: Dry season, perfect beach weather, 25–30°C
- **April–May**: Hot and humid (35°C+), pre-monsoon season
- **June–September**: Monsoon season, lush green but heavy rain
- **Budget tip**: June–September is 40-50% cheaper for hotels and flights

## Top Beaches
- **Baga Beach**: Popular and lively with beach shacks, water sports, and nightlife
- **Calangute Beach**: Widest beach, full of vendors and restaurants
- **Anjuna Beach**: Famous for flea markets on Wednesdays, hippie vibe
- **Vagator Beach**: Scenic cliffs, quieter and more scenic
- **Palolem Beach**: Southern Goa, crescent shaped and very clean, best for swimming
- **Agonda Beach**: Very peaceful, great for yoga retreats

## Historic Attractions
- **Fort Aguada**: 17th century Portuguese fort, lighthouse, excellent sea views
- **Chapora Fort**: Featured in Bollywood films, great sunset viewpoint
- **Basilica of Bom Jesus**: UNESCO World Heritage Site, houses St. Francis Xavier's remains
- **Se Cathedral**: Largest church in Asia, built in 1619
- **Goa State Museum**: Local history, sculptures, and bronze artifacts

## Nature & Activities
- **Dudhsagar Falls**: 310-meter waterfall, best visited Oct–Jan by jeep safari
- **Mollem National Park**: Wildlife sanctuary, spice plantation tours
- **Backwater Kayaking**: Mandovi River, sunset cruises available
- **Scuba Diving**: Grand Island is the best spot — visibility up to 15 meters in Nov–Feb

## Local Food
- **Goan Fish Curry**: Tangy coconut milk-based curry with kingfish or pomfret
- **Prawn Balchão**: Spicy pickled prawn dish
- **Bebinca**: Traditional Goan dessert layered cake
- **Feni**: Local cashew or coconut spirit — Goa's signature drink
- **Best areas to eat**: Mapusa market, Baga beach shacks, Panjim's Latin Quarter

## Budget Guide
- **Budget travelers**: ₹2,000–3,500/day (hostel + local food)
- **Mid-range**: ₹3,500–7,000/day (3-star hotel + restaurants)
- **Luxury**: ₹7,000–20,000/day (beach resorts + fine dining)
- **Off-season (June–Sept)**: 40% cheaper across all categories

## Transport
- Rent a scooter/motorcycle (₹300–400/day) for maximum flexibility
- Taxis use app or negotiate fixed rates (₹200–600 for short trips)
- Goa Miles and Ola operate in major areas
- Airport: Dabolim (GOI) or Manohar International Airport (new)

## Tips & Advice
- Book beach shacks directly for better prices vs. hotels
- Carry cash; many smaller restaurants and shops don't accept cards
- Sunscreen and mosquito repellent are essential
- Legal reminder: Nudity is banned on all Goa beaches despite myths
