# Delhi Travel Guide

## Overview
Delhi, India's capital territory, is a blend of ancient history and modern life. Home to some of India's most iconic Mughal-era monuments, excellent street food, and the largest metro system in Asia. Best visited between October and March.

## Best Time to Visit
- **October–March**: Ideal weather 10–25°C, festivals like Diwali and Holi
- **April–June**: Very hot (40°C+), not ideal
- **July–September**: Monsoon, humid but green
- **Budget tip**: February is shoulder season with lower hotel rates

## Top Historic Attractions
- **Red Fort (Lal Qila)**: UNESCO site, Mughal masterpiece built in 1638, must-visit at night for light show
- **Qutub Minar**: World's tallest brick minaret, UNESCO site from the 12th century
- **Humayun's Tomb**: Inspired the Taj Mahal, tranquil gardens around it
- **India Gate**: 42m war memorial, nearby lawns perfect for evening picnics
- **Jama Masjid**: India's largest mosque, built by Shah Jahan in 1656
- **Lotus Temple**: Bahai temple shaped like a lotus flower, peaceful setting

## Neighborhoods
- **Old Delhi (Shahjahanabad)**: Historic walled city, Chandni Chowk market, street food paradise
- **Connaught Place (CP)**: Commercial hub, good restaurants and shopping
- **Hauz Khas Village**: Artsy neighborhood with cafés, galleries, and a lake
- **Lajpat Nagar**: Great for textiles and affordable shopping
- **Dilli Haat**: Open-air craft bazaar from all Indian states

## Food
- **Chole Bhature**: Fluffy fried bread with spiced chickpeas — Delhi's breakfast staple
- **Paratha Wali Gali**: Famous alley in Old Delhi with stuffed parathas
- **Butter Chicken**: Originated in Delhi (Moti Mahal restaurant in Daryaganj)
- **Kebabs**: Ghalib Kebab Corner and Karim's in Old Delhi are legendary
- **Daulat Ki Chaat**: Winter-only light foam dessert found in Chandni Chowk
- **Nihari**: Slow-cooked meat stew, best for breakfast in Old Delhi

## Budget Guide
- **Budget**: ₹2,500–4,000/day (hostel + street food + metro)
- **Mid-range**: ₹4,000–8,000/day (3-star + restaurants + auto)
- **Luxury**: ₹8,000–25,000/day (5-star + fine dining + cab)
- **Museum entry**: Most monuments ₹35–500 (foreigners pay 10-20x more)

## Transport
- **Delhi Metro**: Best way to get around — cheap, fast, air-conditioned (₹10–60 per trip)
- **Auto-rickshaw**: Use Ola/Uber for fair pricing
- **Cycle Rickshaw**: Great for Old Delhi exploration

## Tips & Advice
- Start early visits to monuments (opens 7am) to beat the heat and crowds
- Keep valuables secure in Chandni Chowk area
- Air quality alert: Nov–Jan often has severe AQI — carry a mask
- Delhi Belly is real — stick to boiled water and well-cooked food initially
- The Delhi Pass gives entry to multiple monuments at a discounted price
