# Bangalore Travel Guide

## Overview
Bangalore (Bengaluru) is India's Silicon Valley — India's startup capital and pub culture hub. Blessed with a year-round pleasant climate, great dining scene, craft beer culture, and good connectivity. Known as the "Garden City" for its parks and greenery.

## Best Time to Visit
- **Year-round**: The climate is incredibly consistent (20–30°C always)
- **October–February**: Occasional rains, very pleasant
- **March–May**: Slightly warm but still very manageable compared to other Indian cities
- **Best deals**: August–September, slightly rainy, fewer tourists

## Top Attractions
- **Lalbagh Botanical Garden**: 240 acres of lush gardens with 1,800 plant species, iconic glass house
- **Cubbon Park**: 300-acre urban lung, great for morning walks and cycling
- **Bangalore Palace**: Tudor-style palace built in 1887, ornate interiors
- **ISKCON Temple**: Large and beautifully built Krishna temple, popular with tourists
- **Vidhana Soudha**: Neo-Dravidian style state legislature building, lit up beautifully at night
- **Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace**: Intricate Indo-Saracenic architecture
- **Bannerghatta National Park**: Wildlife safari and butterfly conservatory

## Neighborhoods & Culture
- **Koramangala**: Food lover's paradise, cafes, restaurants, startups
- **Indiranagar**: Upscale area, great bars and international cuisine  
- **Church Street**: Books, cafes, and the heart of the old British Bangalore
- **Malleswaram**: Traditional Bangalore character, authentic eateries
- **MG Road & Brigade Road**: Shopping, multiplexes, and nightlife

## Food
- **Masala Dosa**: Bangalore is the king of South Indian breakfast — try at MTR or Vidyarthi Bhavan
- **Bisi Bele Bath**: Spiced lentil rice dish, uniquely Bangalorean
- **Filter Coffee**: Strong decoction coffee in traditional steel glass-tumbler
- **Craft Beer & Pubs**: Toit, Arbor Brewing, and Windmills Craftworks are iconic
- **CTR Sponge Dosa**: Legendary pure-ghee dosa at Central Tiffin Room in Malleswaram
- **Karnataka Thali**: Complete meal at places like Brahmin's Coffee Bar or Shivaji Military Hotel

## Budget Guide
- **Budget**: ₹2,500–4,000/day (PG accommodation + local eateries)
- **Mid-range**: ₹4,000–8,000/day (hotel + restaurant meals)
- **Luxury**: ₹8,000–20,000/day (5-star or boutique hotels)
- **Craft beer pints**: ₹200–400 at microbreweries

## Transport
- **Namma Metro**: Purple and Green lines cover major areas, very efficient
- **BMTC Bus**: Extensive bus network but complex for tourists
- **Auto**: Use Rapido or negotiate, autos don't use meters typically
- **Cab**: Ola and Uber are heavily used and reliable
- **Airport (KIA)**: 40km from city, Vayu Vajra (KIAS bus) is cost-effective

## Tips & Advice
- Traffic can be brutal — plan extra time for any road journey
- Cubbon Park and Lalbagh are best visited on weekday mornings
- Bengaluru has outstanding live music, comedy, and art events — check Zomato/District
- The startup culture means co-working spaces are everywhere and great for remote work
- Helmet is mandatory for scooter/bike rentals — police strictly enforce it
